Reflection on Practical 9: Modelling Infections

What I learned most:
Through building the probabilistic SIR models, I learned that even simple assumptions (random infection/recovery) can generate complex, realistic epidemic patterns. The spatial model particularly showed how local interactions create emergent global spread patterns.

Biggest challenge:
Debugging the 2D spatial model was tricky. I had to carefully handle boundary conditions and synchronous updates to prevent cells from being infected multiple times in one time step.

Future improvement:
I'd add an "Exposed" (latent) state to create an SEIR model, showing how incubation periods affect outbreak timing and severity.

Overall experience:
This practical made abstract epidemiological concepts tangible. Seeing the code produce different outbreak curves each run highlighted the importance of stochasticity in real-world disease modeling.
